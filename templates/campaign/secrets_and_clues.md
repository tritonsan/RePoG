# Secrets And Clues

Campaign id: `replace_me`

Use this file for short, flexible secrets and clues. Do not lock a clue to one
NPC, one object, or one required action unless the fiction truly requires it.

Use `knowledge_boundaries.md` for the exact list of who knows each fact and
which proper nouns are protected.

Each line should be a useful fact that can surface through many channels:
conversation, rumor, document, visible evidence, mistake, consequence,
environmental detail, or player ingenuity.

## Status Convention

- `gm_only`: true behind the screen, not yet available.
- `foreshadowable`: can be hinted through evidence, not named as confirmed.
- `pc_known`: player character can act on it.
- `companion_known`: a companion knows it; the player character may not.
- `npc_known`: an NPC or faction knows it through a believable source.
- `revealed`: established to the player and character.

## Active Secrets And Clues

- `S1 | gm_only | Short fact the player might discover. | known_by: GM |
  safe wording: vague clue | possible channels: NPC, place, document,
  consequence | reveal trigger: player action that exposes it`

## Revealed

- `S0 | revealed | Move revealed clues here with where/how they surfaced and
  who now knows it.`

## Guidance

- Keep clues short.
- Separate the fact from its delivery method.
- If the player finds a clever route, let an appropriate clue surface.
- Avoid making one cryptic NPC the only path to understanding.
- Do not use protected names before they are revealed. Use safe wording from
  `knowledge_boundaries.md`.
