# Appearance Guide

Campaign id: `replace_me`

Use this guide when creating player characters, companions, T2+ NPCs, major
locations, factions, ships, bases, or any element that may later receive a
generated visual.

The goal is middle-detail appearance: more useful than a one-line look, but
not an anatomical catalog. Appearance should help the GM keep continuity,
stage scenes, and write image prompts without overloading play.

## Detail Tiers

- T0 incidental: no durable appearance record.
- T1 minor named: 1 to 2 lines with first-glance read, one visible marker, and
  one mannerism or sensory tell.
- T2 supporting: use a compact appearance card.
- T3 major: use a fuller image-ready appearance card, plus visual continuity
  notes if an accepted image exists.

## Character Appearance Card

Use these fields for the player, companions, and important NPCs:

- First-glance read: what someone notices in three seconds.
- Silhouette and build: height impression, frame, posture, movement.
- Face, hair, and eyes: only the details that matter at the table.
- Clothing and gear: ordinary outfit, signature pieces, carried tools,
  symbols, status markers, wear, damage, or cleanliness.
- Marks and history traces: scars, tattoos, missing pieces, calluses,
  weathering, ritual marks, age signs, or other readable history.
- Sensory tell: voice quality, scent, sound, texture, or presence.
- Mannerism: one playable physical habit.
- Image-generation anchor: a compact prompt seed that can recreate the look.
- Changeable details: clothes, injuries, disguise, mood, aging, travel wear.
- Do not change: accepted visual canon that should stay stable.

## Location Appearance Card

Use these fields for important places:

- First-glance read: what the place feels like before inspection.
- Exterior or approach: shape, entrance, signage, streets, landscape, weather.
- Interior or primary area: layout, zones, light, ceiling, surfaces, crowd.
- Secondary areas: upstairs, back rooms, docks, alleys, basement, shrine,
  office, yard, or hidden-adjacent spaces the player may reach.
- Navigation landmarks: what helps the player understand where things are.
- Key props and affordances: objects the player can use, inspect, hide behind,
  steal, break, bargain over, or misunderstand.
- Sound, smell, and motion: the place's living atmosphere.
- Texture and wear: age, maintenance, damage, cleanliness, material reality.
- Image-generation anchor: a compact prompt seed for future visuals.
- Hidden visual facts: GM-only details that must not appear in player-facing
  images yet.
- Changeable details: damage, weather, crowd state, lighting, occupation,
  repairs, signs of conflict.

## Faction Visual Identity

For T2+ factions, define only what helps play:

- public visual identity;
- symbol, colors, uniform, material culture, or tools;
- typical member look;
- elite or specialist variation;
- places or vehicles associated with the faction;
- visual tells outsiders can notice;
- hidden symbols or marks that should not appear before discovery.

## Boundaries

- Do not default to explicit sexual anatomy, fetish detail, or invasive body
  cataloging. If an adult campaign needs private erotic description, keep it
  outside ordinary player-facing appearance and within the campaign's content
  boundaries.
- Do not define protected or spoiler visual facts before the player discovers
  them.
- Do not copy a real person's likeness, a copyrighted character design, a
  specific artist style, or a protected logo unless the Designer explicitly
  provides a safe policy.
- Keep appearance playable. If a detail will not help narration, continuity,
  interaction, or visual generation, cut it.

## Review Questions

- Can the GM describe this character or place consistently three sessions from
  now?
- Can an image prompt be written from this without inventing the whole look?
- Are the stable details separated from changeable clothing, injury, disguise,
  or lighting?
- Does the description reveal only what the player can safely know?
