# Progression And Rewards

Campaign id: `replace_me`

Progression is how player action changes character capability, relationships,
access, reputation, and the world. Do not treat upgrade as a random prize. An
upgrade is the table-visible consequence of what the player actually did.

## Progression Philosophy

- Reward the behavior the campaign wants to see.
- Connect every meaningful reward to fiction: who taught it, what changed,
  what resource was gained, who noticed, and what future pressure answers it.
- Prefer new choices, access, identity, and world reaction over simple number
  inflation.
- Use OOC upgrade check-ins at clean closure points. Keep them table-facing,
  not technical.
- Allied or companion NPCs can advance too, but their growth should follow
  their own participation, wounds, loyalties, training, and choices.

## Closure Levels

### Beat

- Trigger: a small scene beat, clue, bargain, fight, or consequence resolves.
- Default reward: no formal upgrade; maybe a fictional opening, clue, contact
  reaction, or temporary edge.
- OOC check-in: usually none.

### Session

- Trigger: a play session or coherent scene chain ends.
- Default reward: minor milestone.
- Player-facing OOC: quick review and one small adjustment or short-term
  advantage when earned.
- Good rewards: temporary edge, small resource, contact warmth, relationship
  shift, trait/aspect wording tweak, clue consolidation, or recovery space.

### Scenario

- Trigger: a mission, job, local problem, important threat, or clear objective
  closes.
- Default reward: significant milestone.
- Player-facing OOC: offer 2 to 3 upgrade directions and ask the player to
  choose.
- Good rewards: stat/skill improvement within budget, new or improved special
  capability, reputation tag, faction access, useful item, contact, companion
  bond, base access, map/lore unlock, or durable resource.

### Arc

- Trigger: a major antagonist, faction issue, personal conflict, island, route,
  or long dramatic question changes state.
- Default reward: major milestone.
- Player-facing OOC: run a full closure review and choose a character upgrade,
  at least one world/faction/access change, and any companion advancement that
  was earned.
- Good rewards: level band increase, stat cap/budget increase, signature
  capability growth, faction status, base/crew upgrade, companion promotion,
  world-state change, new route, public title, feared reputation, or major
  identity/trait change.

### Campaign

- Trigger: the whole campaign premise resolves or transforms.
- Default reward: legacy/epilogue advancement.
- Player-facing OOC: decide endings, legacies, permanent world changes, and
  what the next campaign inherits.

## End-Of-Closure Review Questions

Ask these in Designer/OOC table language at session, scenario, and arc closure:

- What did the player choose that changed the situation?
- What did the character learn about the world, themself, or another person?
- What threat, obstacle, debt, or pressure was overcome or transformed?
- What resource, contact, item, place, route, secret, or relationship was
  gained?
- What personal goal, belief, bond, flaw, or identity changed?
- What did the world notice?
- Which faction, enemy, ally, or companion now reacts differently?
- What new problem answers the character's growth?

## Player Motivation Lens

Use the player's visible preferences to make upgrades feel alive:

- power: new capability, stronger stat, better tool, clearer build path;
- tactics: new strategic option, enemy weakness, preparation asset;
- story: new thread, dramatic consequence, changed issue;
- roleplay: trait/aspect/bond change, relationship turn, inner conflict;
- exploration: new map, route, lore, secret, place, or truth;
- social: faction access, title, renown, fear, respect, contact network;
- collection: unique item, recipe, artifact, identity, ship/base feature;
- mastery: training, refinement, reliability, reduced cost, better counterplay.

Do not assume the motivation forever. Infer it from repeated player choices and
ask during OOC upgrade check-ins when uncertain.

## Reward Pool

Pick rewards from the fiction first, then fit the category.

- mechanical_upgrade: stat, skill, level band, cap, or reliability increase;
- temporary_boost: one-use edge, inspiration, reroll, advantage, favor, or safe
  recovery window;
- new_ability: new special capability, technique, spell, hack, stunt, craft,
  contact method, or social power;
- reputation: fame, fear, wanted status, title, rumor, or public mark;
- faction_access: permission, safe passage, discount, agent, protection, or
  restricted information;
- base_upgrade: room, safehouse, ship, workshop, lab, shrine, office, crew
  support, or production capacity;
- companion_contact: new ally, improved bond, loyalty shift, or trained helper;
- item_artifact: tool, weapon, document, map, charm, device, resource, or
  symbol of authority;
- map_lore_unlock: route, truth, recipe, ritual, weakness, secret, or hidden
  location;
- trait_change: aspect, belief, goal, flaw, bond, scar, trauma, oath, or
  identity wording;
- world_state_change: issue resolved, location changed, faction move, law
  altered, route opened, enemy weakened, or threat escalated.

## Fiction Binding

Before finalizing any reward, answer:

- Where did it come from?
- Who gave, taught, unlocked, witnessed, or paid for it?
- What did it cost?
- Who is pleased, afraid, insulted, or threatened by it?
- How does it change the next session's pressure?
- Does it need downtime, training, recovery, crafting, travel, or a scene?

## Balance Check

Before presenting upgrade choices:

- Is this reward temporary or permanent?
- Is it individual, companion, faction, base, or world-level?
- Does it increase raw power, access, recognition, agency, or identity?
- Does it fit the closure level?
- Does it overshadow existing capabilities?
- Does the next threat or social pressure need to respond?
- Does it make the game richer instead of shorter?

## Companion And Ally Advancement

Companions and allied NPCs can advance when they:

- participated meaningfully;
- took risk or suffered cost;
- learned from the player or the situation;
- changed loyalty, confidence, role, reputation, or capability;
- gained equipment, access, training, or a new relationship.

Companion rewards may include:

- capability growth;
- clearer role in the group;
- improved trust or loyalty;
- new fear, scar, bond, oath, or ambition;
- faction access;
- equipment or resource;
- promotion from contact to companion or companion to major ally.

Do not make allied NPCs static reward containers. They keep agency. The player
may influence their growth, but the NPC's own motive and experience shape the
final result.

## OOC Upgrade Check-In Script

Use table-facing language:

"This arc closes here. Let's take a short advancement pause: what did you learn,
what changed, and how do you want that to show up in the character or the world?"

Then present 2 to 3 clear upgrade directions tied to what happened. Avoid
technical file language.
