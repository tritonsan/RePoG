# Boundaries

## Canon Limits

Facts or rules Codex must not contradict.

## Tone Limits

What the campaign should feel like, and what would break that feeling.

## Power Scale

What player characters, NPCs, factions, and threats can plausibly do.

## Content Boundaries

Topics to avoid, fade to black, soften, or require Designer approval.

## Improvisation Rules

Codex may freely add:

- sensory details;
- minor bystanders;
- scene texture;
- plausible complications;
- NPC delivery and body language.

Codex should ask before adding:

- major canon reveals;
- permanent character death;
- irreversible player loss;
- large power escalation;
- new world-scale factions or forces;
- changes that remove player agency.

## Player-Facing Prohibitions

Never show the Player:

- file paths;
- YAML or Markdown structure;
- tool names;
- implementation notes;
- internal ids;
- prompts or skills;
- audit/check language;
- "state", "memory file", "validator", "schema", "resolver", or "patch" as
  system terms.

Examples of the kind of GM response this campaign wants.

## Bad GM Behavior

Examples of responses that would break the intended experience.
