# Research Dossier

Use this file before building durable world facts. It records whether the
campaign needs outside research, what was learned, what remains uncertain, and
which rules Codex must respect during worldbuilding and play.

## Research Status

- Status: `needed_pending`
- Mode: `fully_original`
- Last reviewed:
- Research performed by:
- Web search used: no

Allowed statuses:

- `not_needed`: the campaign can rely on player answers and ordinary genre
  assumptions.
- `needed_pending`: research should happen before durable worldbuilding.
- `researched`: research was performed and source notes are recorded below.
- `user_supplied`: the Designer provided the authoritative source material.
- `unavailable`: research was needed but not available; assumptions must be
  explicit and conservative.

Allowed modes:

- `canon_existing_world`
- `real_world_specific`
- `genre_adjacent`
- `fully_original`
- `user_supplied_homebrew`

## Source Scope

What is being researched: canon setting, real-world period/place/profession,
genre reference, science/technology basis, cultural model, or user-provided
homebrew material.

## Canon Or Realism Policy

How close the campaign should stay to source material, real-world constraints,
or player-provided facts.

## Story Affordances

What kinds of stories naturally fit this world.

## Hard Boundaries

Facts, events, powers, technologies, institutions, or tones that would break
the world if introduced without Designer approval.

## Physical And Metaphysical Rules

The important rules of physics, magic, powers, travel, medicine, death,
communication, and causality.

## Social, Political, And Economic Structure

How authority, class, law, money, reputation, institutions, and everyday life
work.

## Power, Technology, Or Expertise Limits

What ordinary people can do, what trained people can do, what rare specialists
can do, and what is legendary, illegal, impossible, or too costly.

## Common Conflicts And Institutions

Recurring pressures, organizations, roles, and conflicts that can generate
playable situations.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

Source-breaking assumptions, genre cliches, tonal mistakes, or realism errors
Codex should avoid.

## Open Questions For Session 0

Questions Codex must ask the Designer instead of silently inventing an answer.

## Use In Play

Rules for consulting this dossier during play:

- Do not turn uncertain research into durable canon.
- Ask the Designer before changing source scope, canon level, physical rules,
  power limits, or realism assumptions.
- If a proposed scene fact conflicts with this dossier, revise the scene or ask
  for a Designer ruling.

## Source Notes

Record short notes and links here when web search or external references were
used. Keep quotations short. Prefer paraphrase plus URL.
