# Scene Entry And Opening Playbook

Use this playbook for the first campaign scene, a return after interruption, a
new location, or a deliberate scene reframe. Do not load it for every turn.

## Sources

- First opening: read `opening_brief.md`, the relevant player/location notes,
  and only the player-safe rows of `knowledge_boundaries.md`.
- `first_session.md` is preparation, not a second current opening. Transfer its
  chosen start to `opening_brief.md` and mark it `materialized`; mark it
  `consumed` after that opening is used in play.
- Resume: use `current_state.yaml.scene_frame.resume_anchor`; do not reconstruct
  the last beat from broad history.
- Post-arc: also require the carried-forward truth in `next_act_prep.md`.

## Frame The Scene

Choose the scene mode before drafting and combine:

`baseline routine + scene mode + current disruption + natural presence + arrival/action`

Establish only what the character can perceive or already knows:

1. where they are and what kind of place it is;
2. what was happening here before their attention arrived;
3. what is different, interrupted, or newly relevant now;
4. one reaction point at which they can act;
5. neutral affordances that exist whether or not they take the obvious hook.

Do not open with a campaign encyclopedia, a packaged conclusion, or a quest
menu. A quiet, empty, closed, damaged, or routine location can still be alive
when its condition has a cause and practical consequences.

## First Campaign Opening

Give arrival context so the character does not appear from nowhere. Reveal the
immediate situation and one active pressure without explaining its hidden
cause. Let useful lore surface later through action, behavior, consequences,
rumor, or focused questions.

The opening should make at least one natural action legible without implying
that it is mandatory. If the Player refuses the apparent hook, continue from
the place's routine, relationships, pressures, and neutral affordances.

## Resume

Restate only enough of the resume anchor to restore orientation: the last
Player action, its direct result, the last actor/world move, and the concrete
moment where control returned. Do not recap the entire scene and do not advance
the fiction while reminding the Player.

## Location Independence

Treat local routine and ordinary activity as causal context, not decorative
quota. Ask internally:

- What work, care, traffic, conflict, maintenance, or rest would happen here
  if the Player never arrived?
- Who belongs here now, who is absent, and why?
- What does the current disruption change for local people?
- What can the Player do here that is unrelated to the primary pressure?

Show only the answers needed to make the present choice intelligible. Local
noise, a false lead, an extra NPC, or a clue is optional; never add one merely
to make the location look busy.

## Scene Frame

At a durable entry or checkpoint, keep a stable `scene_id` and record the mode,
prior local process, current disruption, last causal beat, up to three pending
consequences, and a concrete resume anchor. Do not rewrite this frame on soft
turns.

## Knowledge And Source Gate

Keep GM-only causes and protected names out of Player Mode. If a source-sensitive
fact is unresolved, use already established safe wording or pause in Designer
Mode before making it durable. Never use the opening voice to smuggle in facts
the character could not know.

## Draft Shape

Put the nearest actionable situation before secondary atmosphere. Vary length
with scene function. End on a visible action, obstacle, offer, change, or open
space where the Player naturally wants to respond. Offer explicit options only
when requested, when the profile calls for them, or when a stuck Player needs a
gentle restart.
