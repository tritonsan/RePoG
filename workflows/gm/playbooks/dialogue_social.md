# Dialogue And Social Playbook

Use this playbook when an NPC conversation, influence attempt, negotiation,
relationship turn, or social reading is central.

## NPC Agency Card

For every active T2/T3 NPC, reason from the character note's compact card:

- local role and independent project;
- current mundane task;
- decision principle under pressure;
- mistaken belief or repeating error;
- line they will not cross;
- obligation to someone other than the Player;
- speech rhythm and social tactic;
- routine and availability;
- next move if ignored;
- evaluation trigger and visible consequence channel.

The card is a decision aid, not prose to recite. Let the NPC continue doing
ordinary work, protect their own time, and have relationships that do not orbit
the Player.

## Model-Only Contrast Pass

When creating or promoting a T2/T3 NPC, compare them with the two most similar
active NPCs across six axes:

1. local role;
2. desire or independent project;
3. response to risk;
4. social tactic;
5. speech rhythm;
6. moral boundary.

If four or more axes substantially match, redesign at least two axes before
using the NPC. This is semantic GM judgment. Do not call or create a checker,
score file, or per-turn report for it.

## Presence Gate

Before a recurring NPC appears, establish their last location, elapsed time,
plausible route, routine/availability, current task, reason here, and how recent
history shapes their posture. A useful NPC is not automatically nearby. With a
weak route or reason, use absence, delay, a believable message, another local
person, or let the Player seek them.

## Knowledge Protocol

An NPC may state a fact only if they witnessed it, heard it through a credible
channel, learned it through their role or local knowledge, or inferred it from
evidence available to them. Bind sharp conclusions to the evidence source.

Weak evidence produces uncertainty, testing, a partial guess, a plausible
mistake, or watchfulness—not GM-level accuracy. Separate:

- what they know;
- what they suspect;
- what they want the Player to believe;
- what they are unwilling to say.

Do not let one NPC's knowledge silently leak to companions, factions, or other
NPCs.

## Speech And Posture

Suspicion is one posture, not the default. Use warm, busy, indifferent,
practical, frightened, proud, greedy, official, helpful, awkward, or conflicted
behavior when it follows from the person and scene.

Ordinary people usually speak ordinarily. Keep key information separate from
personality so a clue does not require deciphering a theatrical monologue.
Prefer at most one highly polished or aphoristic line in a scene unless the
NPC's established voice genuinely supports more.

Preserve distinct word choice, rhythm, directness, status behavior, social
tactic, and metaphor family. Do not give every clever NPC the narrator's
favorite gestures, pauses, or metaphors.

## Resolve Social Action

Honor the Player's actual approach and leverage. Decide what the NPC wants,
fears, can afford, knows, and controls. Routine requests that fit the
relationship and cost little can succeed cleanly. Use resistance only for a
real conflict of interest, uncertainty, risk, or meaningful cost.

Social success changes observable behavior—access, disclosure, terms,
commitment, posture, or timing. It does not let the GM declare the player
character convinced, trusting, ashamed, frightened, or morally persuaded.

## Conversation Beat

Resolve the Player's last line or action first. Let the NPC answer while
pursuing their own immediate objective, then change one concrete social fact or
affordance. End before the Player character's reply. Do not append an unrelated
threat or mystery merely to keep the conversation moving.
