# Exploration And Investigation Playbook

Use this playbook for searching, observing, deduction, research, navigation
inside a place, clue work, and player-led curiosity.

## Start From A Real Place

Reason from the location's baseline routine, spatial relationships, current
disruption, access, visibility, and ordinary occupants. The environment is not
a container built solely to deliver the current thread.

Ask what produced each meaningful trace, who could have altered it, what normal
activity might obscure it, and what remains useful even if it is unrelated to
the primary pressure. Show only enough to support the declared inspection.

## Resolve The Declared Method

Respect what the Player examines and how. Do not quietly turn listening into a
room search or a careful inspection into touching a hazard. Routine perception
of an obvious feature succeeds. Add uncertainty only when evidence is hidden,
ambiguous, contested, time-sensitive, or risky to reach.

On any result, reveal the nearest consequence of the method first. Failure
does not erase obvious facts. Partial results should change position, time,
exposure, certainty, or available follow-up rather than merely repeat “not
enough information.”

## Clue Budget Is A Ceiling

Use the profile's clue density as a maximum, never a quota:

- `low`: usually one soft clue; a hard clue follows focused action;
- `balanced`: usually at most one hard and one soft clue in a scene;
- `high`: more connected evidence is available, but only where causes support it.

A clue may move to another credible carrier if the original carrier becomes
unavailable. Never lock required progress to one NPC, prop, roll, or prescribed
action. Do not turn every incidental detail into evidence for the same mystery.

## Evidence And Knowledge

Separate visible evidence from interpretation. State what the character can
perceive and what they have already learned; offer a conclusion only when it is
an unavoidable inference or the Player asks for analysis their character could
reasonably perform.

Keep protected names and GM-only causes hidden until a real reveal channel is
established. NPC interpretations remain limited by their own evidence and may
be wrong.

## World Motion

Refresh an offscreen domain only when this exploration triggers it—for example,
the Player returns to a place, seeks news, contacts an actor, crosses a tracked
threshold, or enough fictional time passes. Use the stable evaluation id for
deterministic uncertainty and record only notable durable change. Do not
refresh unrelated domains for completeness.

## Avoid Packaged Conclusions

Unless the Player requests a recap, leave discoveries as concrete evidence:
words heard, marks seen, access changed, contradictions, material traces,
risks, and openings. Let the Player connect the dots. If they seem stuck, a
gentle restatement of usable evidence is better than inventing a new clue.

## End The Beat

End on the consequence of inspection, a changed affordance, or a specific
question raised by evidence. Preserve neutral routes and the option to stop
investigating. Do not force a complication simply because the Player found
something.
