# Breather And Aftermath Playbook

Use this playbook when pressure has naturally eased, consequences are settling,
or the Player seeks safe time for recovery, companionship, curiosity, or a
small personal goal.

## Purpose

A breather is playable space, not filler and not a promise that the world has
stopped. It lets urgency recede when the fiction supports it and gives the
Player authority over how long to remain. Do not impose a fixed turn count.

## Natural Entry

Consider `breather` or `aftermath` mode when:

- a major pressure or conflict resolves;
- the character reaches a reasonably safe place;
- travel or downtime creates spare time;
- wounds, equipment, shelter, or a base need care;
- an important relationship has room to breathe;
- the Player deliberately creates safety or asks to rest.

Use `play_profile.yaml.narration.breather_frequency` as a bias, not a schedule.
`sparse` waits for strong openings, `balanced` accepts ordinary natural
openings, and `generous` protects more low-pressure space when plausible.

## Aftermath

Show what the preceding event changed: physical cost, altered routine, access,
public reaction, private relationship, material damage, or emotional evidence
visible in behavior. Do not dictate the player character's meaning or feeling.

Aftermath may remain tense. It becomes a breather when immediate danger has
receded enough that the Player can choose non-urgent action.

## Offer Space, Not A Menu

Reveal a few ordinary affordances through the scene rather than listing tasks:
food, sleep, repair, bathing, conversation, training, a personal project,
companion time, low-stakes exploration, entertainment, or solitude. Choose only
what the location, people, resources, and culture support.

NPCs may pursue their own chores and wants. Conversation does not need to
deliver a clue, quest, confession, or warning to justify itself.

## Sustain The Breather

If the Player stays, let time, routine, relationship, and small projects
develop. Resolve each declared action normally. Do not create suspicion,
complication, arrival, alarm, or sudden danger merely because several calm
turns have passed.

Existing distant pressures may remain visible as weather, schedules, rumors,
limited resources, or remembered commitments without demanding immediate
action. Quiet, comfort, awkwardness, humor, mourning, or mundane work can carry
the scene.

## Exit Policy

Follow `play_profile.yaml.narration.breather_exit_policy`:

- `player_led`: exit only when the Player chooses a new goal or follows an
  affordance into another scene.
- `player_led_with_established_triggers`: prefer Player choice; an already
  established event may arrive only when its trigger is genuinely due and has
  a believable channel.
- `world_led`: established world motion may end the lull more readily, but it
  still needs a prior cause, due trigger, plausible timing, and delivery path.

Never invent a fresh threat solely to close the breather. When a trigger
arrives, show its channel first—a messenger, deadline, visible change, call,
return, or consequence—and let the Player decide how to respond unless direct
physical danger truly removes delay.

## Persistence

Ordinary calm conversation and color remain soft: no writes or checks. Persist
only durable relationship, condition, project, inventory, time, or scene
changes. At the eventual exit, write a scene checkpoint and resume anchor; do
not full-distill solely because the breather ended.
