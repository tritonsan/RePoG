# Example Seed: Fantasy Frontier

This is a generic starter seed for testing RePoG. It is not a complete
campaign and does not depend on any copyrighted setting.

## Pitch

A low-magic frontier valley is reopening after a generation of isolation. Old
roads are being cleared, abandoned shrines are waking, and every settlement has
a different story about why the pass was sealed.

Player fantasy: a capable wanderer, scout, sellsword, healer, scholar, or
exile trying to make a name before larger powers arrive.

## Session 0 Answers

- Genre: grounded frontier fantasy.
- Tone: hopeful but dangerous.
- Scale: valley and mountain pass, expanding later.
- Play focus: travel, local politics, dangerous bargains, old ruins, practical
  survival.
- Magic level: rare, costly, and tied to place.
- Canon policy: original world.
- Avoid: chosen-one destiny, cosmic lore dump, constant combat pressure.
- Campaign length: medium, with room for multiple route-based arcs.

## World Notes

- The old pass is open again, but not safely.
- Three towns want control of the reopened road.
- A mining charter is paying too much for maps of abandoned places.
- Local shrines answer requests, but always ask for a remembered debt.
- Most people are not villains; they are afraid of being left behind.

## Player Seed

The player character arrives with one practical skill, one personal debt, and
one reason to avoid returning home.

Recommended starting level: Local Rookie / Beginner.

Suggested capabilities:

- pathfinding or wilderness survival;
- local healing or folk magic;
- dueling, archery, or shield work;
- negotiation with isolated settlements;
- old-language scholarship.

Each capability should have a limit, risk, and situation where it does not
work.

## First Scene Brief

The character reaches a bridge settlement at dusk. A work crew has stopped
repairing the road because something under the bridge is repeating the names of
people who died before the pass closed.

The scene should open neutrally: show the place, the stopped work, the worried
locals, and the visible choices. Do not explain the full history of the valley
yet.

## Expected Play Style

Use local life first, quest hooks second. Let NPCs have mundane work, fears, and
conflicting needs. Clues should surface through travel, bargains, weathered
objects, rumors, and consequences instead of one mysterious speaker.
