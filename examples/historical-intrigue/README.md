# Example Seed: Historical Intrigue

This is a generic starter seed for testing RePoG. It is not a complete
campaign. It can be run as original historical fiction or adapted to a specific
real-world period after research.

## Pitch

A port city is preparing for a succession ceremony while merchants, foreign
envoys, guild officers, and local families quietly compete over debt, marriage,
grain, shipping rights, and public reputation.

Player fantasy: a clerk, courier, minor noble, physician, bodyguard, translator,
scribe, advocate, performer, or household agent who can move between social
layers.

## Session 0 Answers

- Genre: historical or historical-inspired intrigue.
- Tone: grounded, social, tense, occasionally warm.
- Scale: one city and its surrounding estates.
- Play focus: reputation, secrets, favors, legal constraints, observation, and
  careful choices.
- Mechanics weight: social and investigative first; combat is rare and serious.
- Canon policy: either original city or researched real-world period.
- Avoid: modern slang, anachronistic institutions unless intentionally chosen,
  constant assassination plots.
- Campaign length: medium, centered on a ceremony season.

## World Notes

- Public honor and private debt rarely match.
- The ceremony creates deadlines for every faction.
- Servants, clerks, guards, and messengers often know more than titled people.
- A public scandal can be more dangerous than a blade.
- Accurate historical grounding matters if the campaign uses a real period.

## Player Seed

The player character begins with a household, patron, guild, or legal office
connection that grants access but also creates obligations.

Recommended starting level: Local Rookie / Beginner or Rising / Competent.

Suggested capabilities:

- etiquette and courtly reading;
- language and translation;
- legal or financial records;
- medicine or household knowledge;
- discreet movement through service spaces;
- persuasion, performance, or reputation management.

Each capability should define who respects it, who resents it, and what risk it
creates when used publicly.

## First Scene Brief

The character arrives at a public registry hall on the morning a marriage
contract is supposed to be witnessed. The clerk in charge has not arrived, two
families are pretending not to panic, and a messenger is trying to leave before
anyone asks why the seal on his letter is broken.

Do not open with the full conspiracy. Open with place, social pressure, visible
manners, and the character's immediate access.

## Expected Play Style

Let the city feel busy without making every person suspicious. Some NPCs should
be helpful, bored, vain, proud, frightened, or simply doing their jobs.
Consequences should often be social: access lost, rumors changed, debts called
in, invitations offered, or names protected.
