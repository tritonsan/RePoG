# Survival Lens

Use when scarcity, exposure, route choice, shelter, injury, or recovery should
regularly change what the player can safely attempt.

## Decide

- Which resources are scarce enough to matter, and in what units?
- What time scale, weather, terrain, exposure, fatigue, or shelter rules affect
  decisions?
- How punishing should wounds, loss, failed preparation, and recovery be?
- Is travel abstract, route-based, or strict?
- Which tasks are routine competence, and which require uncertainty or cost?
- When should bookkeeping compress so survival does not become clerical work?

## Tracking Presets To Offer

- Light: coarse time, narrative wounds, abstract travel, and only exceptional
  supply changes. Lowest tracking cost.
- Focused: route travel, tracked wounds, clocks, and a short list of important
  consumables. Moderate tracking cost.
- Strict: quantified inventory, turn/scene time, exposure and wound conditions,
  route state, and explicit recovery. Highest tracking cost.

## Optional Mechanics To Offer

- `strict_consumables` for supplies and consumables;
- `wounds` for injury, fatigue, exposure, hunger, or illness;
- `structured_travel` for travel, weather windows, and deadlines;
- `clocks` for depletion, pursuit, storms, infection, or shelter failure;
- `bounded_resources` for a small number of bounded shared resources;
- `dice_resolution` when uncertainty should be visible at the table.

This lens does not activate strict inventory, hunger, HP, encumbrance, weather
simulation, or dice by itself. Require explicit approval for every tracked
module and name its expected play cost.
