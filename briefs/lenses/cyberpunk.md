# Cyberpunk Lens

Use when technology, concentrated power, identity, surveillance, and unequal
access are central rather than decorative.

## Decide

- Who owns infrastructure, data, augmentation, security, and legal identity?
- What can technology do, who can afford it, and what is its failure mode?
- How are people tracked, profiled, erased, copied, indebted, or excluded?
- What distinguishes public reputation, legal identity, and hidden identity?
- Which corporate, state, criminal, community, or autonomous actors benefit?
- How does pressure become visible through heat, access, debt, scarcity, or
  social consequence?

## Suggested World Material

Define one infrastructure dependency, one access boundary, one surveillance
channel, one everyday adaptation, and one group living with the externalized
cost. Keep hacking and augmentation bounded by source, access, counterplay,
trace, time, and consequence.

## Optional Mechanics To Offer

- `abilities_costs` for implants, programs, access privileges, or bounded techniques;
- `bounded_resources` for charge, bandwidth, credits, favors, or trace capacity;
- `strict_consumables` for scarce gear, ammo, or consumable tech;
- `wounds` for trauma, system damage, or augmentation stress;
- `clocks` for heat, trace, pursuit, exposure, or corporate response;
- `dice_resolution` for contested intrusion or risky high-variance actions.

Do not assume a universal hacking minigame, cyberware slots, hit points, or a
currency ledger unless the Designer approves it.
